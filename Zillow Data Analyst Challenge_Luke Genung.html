Zillow Data Analyst Challenge¶

By Luke Genung Date: 7/24/20

This is a mock take-home data challenge that we could imagine for a company like Zillow.

As a disclaimer, I have never interviewed at Zillow or its competitors (like Trulia, Redfin, etc.). I do not know about their actual hiring practices.

The Challenge¶

The Zillow App sends what are called “To-Do’s” (tasks in the data model) to users (buyers, sellers, real estate agents, transaction coordinators) asking for information - think TurboTax, but for escrow. There are various types of To-Do’s, and when users complete them in the app, it generates efficiency gains from an operational standpoint as it is not necessary to chase the customer down for the information. That is why “To-Do completion %” is one of the most important metrics, and the company is constantly looking for areas to improve these efficiencies.

The Task¶

Extract and analyze the data provided in the accompanying Redshift database, and present the following:

  • Key questions going into the exercise
  • Insights and conclusions drawn from analyzing the data
  • Recommendations on areas of opportunity to increase “To-Do completion %” for each of the user and/or To-Do types
  • Areas of exploration that you would suggest doing as a next step / to dig in and further solidify your recommendations
  • Executive Summary of the exercise. Format of the above output is your choice.

Include everything used to complete the task. This may include SQL scripts, Python code, visualizations, or other outputs.

Overview¶

  • The Zillow app sends "To-Do's" to users asking for information regarding properties and rentals
  • There are various types of To Do's that generate efficiency gains from an operational standpoint
  • To-Do completion % is a key metric, since Zillow is constantly looking for areas to improve these efficiencies

Key Questions¶

  • What levers can we pull to increase To-Do Completion %?
  • Task types:
    • What improvements can be made to increase To-Do Completion for each task type?
    • What task types have the lowest & highest completion?
  • Users & roles:
    • What can improvements can be made to increase To-Do Completion for each user segement / role?
    • What roles have the lowest & highest completion %?
    • What calls-to-action can we give users to increase their To-Do Completion %?

Assumptions¶

  • To-Do completion % is measured as whether a task is completed (column = tasks.completed) on or before its due date (column = tasks.due_date)
    • tasks.status = ‘Complete’ OR ‘Completed by Peer’
  • The data includes dummy records that should be excluded. For example:
    • tasks.name LIKE ‘%test%’ (35 records)
  • Many tasks are assigned to users that do not exist in the users table. For this analysis, I assume all tasks are assigned to a valid user (though unclear whether that is the case). (13,463 records)

        SELECT
            priority,
            type,
            COUNT(*) AS num_complete_tasks
        FROM tasks
        WHERE (status = 'Completed' OR status = 'Completed by Peer')
        AND name NOT LIKE '%test%'
        AND type <> 'NA'
        GROUP BY 1,2
    ), 

    total_tasks AS (
        SELECT
            priority,
            type,
            COUNT(*) AS num_total_tasks
        FROM tasks
        WHERE name NOT LIKE '%test%'
        AND type <> 'NA'
        GROUP BY 1,2
    )

    SELECT
        total_tasks.priority,
        total_tasks.type,
        num_complete_tasks,
        num_total_tasks - num_complete_tasks AS num_incomplete_tasks,
        num_total_tasks,
        ROUND(num_complete_tasks * 100.0 / num_total_tasks, 1) AS task_complete_percent
    FROM total_tasks
    LEFT JOIN complete_tasks ON total_tasks.priority = complete_tasks.priority
    AND total_tasks.type = complete_tasks.type
    ORDER BY total_tasks.priority, task_complete_percent DESC
    """, conn
)

User Summary¶

Buyers and Sellers complete To-Do's at high rate (79% & 84%), though Agents are lagging behind (52%).¶

  1. Buyers and Sellers are completing their To-Do's at a consistent rate month-over-month
  2. However, To-Do Completion for Agents may be declining in recent months

To increase To-Do Completion %, we should engage users with specific marketing campaigns and calls-to-action.¶

  1. 48% of To-Do's are assigned to users that have not onboarded - need users to complete in-app registration
  2. 53% of To-Do's are assigned to users that have not enabled email notifications
  3. 54% of To-Do's are assigned to users that have not verified their phone number
  4. 0% of To-Do's are assigned to users with e-notifications enabled
